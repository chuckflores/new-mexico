  • Kasha-Katuwe Tent Rocks National Monument

    The Tent Rocks National Monument is a geological formation of tall tent like rock columns located about 40 miles south of Santa Fe. The formation was the result of millions of years of erosion. This erosion chiseled away the cone like structured of today.

    We started our journey early, arriving at the park around 10 AM. The beginning of the hike was a gentle gravel path, about 1/4 mile, leading to the Canyon Trail.

    tip: Show up as early as you can and you can have the Canyon Trail to yourself. Because of the narrow canyon walls it's a better experience to take your time and not feel rushed or impeded by other hikers.

    As we entered the Canyon Trail, the tall walls overlooked us. The vegetation started to diminish as the canyon walls narrowed.
