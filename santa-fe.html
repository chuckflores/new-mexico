    Inn at Loretto

    The Inn at Loretto is a real Santa Fe treat. Located downtown it has the distinct Santa Fe adobe architecture mixed with the charm of a luxury cabin in the woods. Service was impeccable. When we arrived we joined the free wine tasting and hung out in the lodge for more drinks and live music. With a fireplace roaring, and feeling the effects of alcohol at high altitudes, it was the perfect quiet night after a long day of travel. Hallelujah (heard song that I love)

    Santa Fe Farmer’s Market

    Any kind of food market is on our travel checklist. Rachel bought some chap stick with only two ingredients, just the same as she made it at her hippy summer camp. We also picked up some chili infused chocolates to enjoy back in our hotel room.

    Teseque Flea Market

    There are great aspects of traveling in the tourist off season - mostly, you don’t have to deal with a bunch of tourists but there are downsides as well. We ran into one of the downsides when we visited the Teseque Flea Market. In the cold December weather, there weren't many vendors but we still enjoyed our time exploring this artistic, sometimes crazy, sometimes creepy - always interesting market.

    Town

    The town of Santa Fe revolves around the city center. There are many churches and old architecture from the cities past.

    Food

    El Chile Toreado

    Unpretentious, deliciously spicy tacos served out of a food truck, eaten on paper plates in our rental car is what travel is about. The amount of meat they load into these tacos is commendable and the spice level is nothing to sneeze at. With a limited selection of toppings, the taco truck is a DIY taco joint where they give you more meat than you've ever seen on a taco and you try to make room for some salsa and pickled onions. Just damn good tacos, people. These tacos were the real deal.

    Jambo Cafe

    Why wouldn't one of the best restaurants in Santa Fe be an African-Caribbean place in a strip mall? After reading lots of reviews and getting a recommendation from our friend Quincy, we decided Jambo Cafe was a must eat stop on our road trip. The food lived up to its reputation. Chef Ahmed Obo obviously loves what he does. We tasted the love and soul in the food and felt the warmth of the neighborhood as Chef Obo stopped by our table welcomed us and chatted us up about his time living in NY.

    We started with the Jerk Chicken Wings and enjoyed Coconut Curry Chicken and Caribbean Goat Stew. We recommend all that we ate but have a feeling you can’t really go wrong here.

    San Marcos Cafe & Feed Store

    After our hike at the Tent Rocks we stopped at this cafe just outside Santa Fe for lunch. There is a feed store connected to the restaurant and peacocks roam the grounds. We sat next to the wood burning fireplace with views of a very large turkey coup.

    We split a cinnamon roll baked in a muffin tin. Chuck had the Huevos Rancheros and Rachel had a bowl of Green Chili. Perfect for a brisk day.

    Drinks

    Arroyo Cafe

    As coffee fans we read that Arroyo Cafe was the spot to get delicious coffee in downtown Santa Fe. We first attempted to get their famous cold brew at 8am before we headed out to Tent Rocks. That attempt was thwarted by the fact that it was 8am and the owner wouldn't be in “until around 10am, maybe.” Our second attempt, we felt was better planned at around 11am but once again, no dice.

    On our third try, success! We drank cold brew espressos while the owner and and a local regular jammed on the guitar and piano. We are so happy we got to experience this cafe!

    Kakawa Chocolate House

    You had me at chocolate house, Kakawa. Sometimes you need a reminder that life is full of simple pleasures and drinking a warm chocolate elixir made with real chocolate as the sun set through the windows, was that reminder for us.
